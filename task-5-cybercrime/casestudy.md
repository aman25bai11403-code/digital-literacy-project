**Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud** UPI fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers trick victims into authorizing unauthorized transactions. It typically happens through "Request Money" scams, where an attacker sends a payment request to the victim under the guise of a refund or a prize. The victim is pressured to enter their UPI PIN, thinking they are _receiving_ money, when they are actually _authorizing_ a transfer out of their own account.

College students are often targeted through fake internship stipends or online marketplace listings. The consequences include immediate financial loss and the compromise of linked bank account details. In a realistic scenario, a student selling a textbook online might be sent a QR code to "scan for payment," only to find their account emptied after entering their PIN.
